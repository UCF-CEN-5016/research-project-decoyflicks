TITLE  
Gradients Do Not Exist for Certain Variables During Model Training

SYMPTOMS  
• Encountering a warning message during model training:  
  "WARNING:tensorflow:Gradients do not exist for variables ['stack_6/block_1/expand_bn/gamma:0', 'stack_6/block_1/expand_bn/beta:0', 'stack_6/block_1/depthwise_conv2d/depthwise_kernel:0', 'stack_6/block_1/depthwise_bn/gamma:0', 'stack_6/block_1/depthwise_bn/beta:0', 'stack_6/block_1/project_bn/gamma:0', 'stack_6/block_1/project_bn/beta:0', 'top_bn/gamma:0', 'top_bn/beta:0'] when minimizing the loss."  
• The warning suggests that a `loss` argument may be missing in the `model.compile()` method.  
• The issue appeared suddenly after previously successful model training.  

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
When the model is trained correctly, gradients should be computed for all trainable variables, and the training process should proceed without warnings.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Load the "efficientdet_d1_coco17_tpu-32" model.  
2. Prepare a custom dataset for training.  
3. Execute the training script using "model_main_tf2.py".  
4. Observe the warning message regarding missing gradients during the training process.  
5. Check the `model.compile()` method to ensure a `loss` argument is provided.
