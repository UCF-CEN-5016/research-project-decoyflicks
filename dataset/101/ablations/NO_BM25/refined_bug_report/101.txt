TITLE  
NaNs Introduced in K After Rotating Queries and Keys

SYMPTOMS  
• K contains a small fraction of NaN values after executing rotary_emb.rotate_queries_and_keys(Q, K).  
• The presence of NaNs in K leads to NaN loss during the transformer operation.  
• The issue persists through subsequent operations in the transformer, propagating NaNs throughout the model.  

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
When the software functions correctly, rotating queries and keys should not introduce any NaN values into K, and the loss should remain finite and well-defined throughout the training process.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Initialize the transformer model and input data.  
2. Prepare the queries (Q) and keys (K) for rotation.  
3. Call the function rotary_emb.rotate_queries_and_keys(Q, K).  
4. Check the contents of K after the function call to verify the presence of NaNs.  
5. Proceed with the transformer operations and observe the loss value for NaNs.
