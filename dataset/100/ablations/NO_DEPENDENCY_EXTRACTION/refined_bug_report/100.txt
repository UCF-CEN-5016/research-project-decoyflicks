TITLE  
Caching frequency results in RuntimeError: Trying to backward through the graph a second time

SYMPTOMS  
• Encountered a `RuntimeError` during the second call to `loss.backward()` in the training process.  
• Error message: `RuntimeError: Trying to backward through the graph a second time (or directly access saved tensors after they have already been freed). Saved intermediate values of the graph are freed when you call .backward() or autograd.grad(). Specify retain_graph=True if you need to backward through the graph a second time or if you need to access saved tensors after calling backward.`  
• The issue arises specifically when the rotary embedding library attempts to read cached frequency values.  
• Commenting out the caching part of the code allows the training process to run without issues.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The rotary embedding should correctly utilize cached frequency values without causing a `RuntimeError` during the second backward pass. The training should proceed smoothly through multiple iterations without needing to recalculate frequencies in every forward pass.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Initialize the rotary embedding library with caching enabled.  
2. Run a training loop that involves multiple iterations, specifically calling `loss.backward()` twice.  
3. Observe the occurrence of the `RuntimeError` during the second iteration of backward propagation.  
4. Comment out the caching logic in the `forward()` method and verify that the training completes without errors.  

By following these steps, the issue can be consistently reproduced, indicating a potential problem with the caching mechanism or its interaction with PyTorch's autograd system.
