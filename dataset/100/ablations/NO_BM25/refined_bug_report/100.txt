TITLE  
Caching frequency results in RuntimeError: Trying to backward through the graph a second time

SYMPTOMS  
• Encountered a `RuntimeError` during the second iteration of `loss.backward()`  
• Error message: `RuntimeError: Trying to backward through the graph a second time (or directly access saved tensors after they have already been freed).`  
• The issue arises specifically when using cached frequency in the rotary embedding implementation.  
• Commenting out the caching logic allows the training process to proceed without errors.  

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The library should allow for the use of cached frequencies without encountering a `RuntimeError` during the second call of `loss.backward()`. The expected behavior is that the model can successfully compute gradients through multiple backward passes when caching frequencies is implemented correctly.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Initialize the rotary embedding library with the appropriate parameters.  
2. Implement caching of frequencies in the `forward()` method of the rotary embedding.  
3. Execute the training loop, ensuring that `loss.backward()` is called twice.  
4. Observe the occurrence of the `RuntimeError` during the second call to `loss.backward()`.  
5. Comment out the caching logic and rerun the training loop to confirm that the error does not occur.
