TITLE  
RandAugment magnitude standard deviation is incorrectly fixed at 1

SYMPTOMS  
• The applied standard deviation for RandAugment is always 1, regardless of the intended configuration.  
• There is a potential missing multiplication factor (`level_std*`) in the code that may lead to this issue.  
• The behavior results in a lack of variability in augmentations, affecting the model's training process.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The RandAugment implementation should allow for a variable magnitude standard deviation that is influenced by the `level_std` parameter, enabling a range of augmentations during model training.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Review the RandAugment implementation in the specified file: https://github.com/tensorflow/models/blob/9fb4a4fbcd888616586ceffa1b8806ffe1126f54/official/vision/ops/augment.py#L1566.  
2. Check the code for the calculation of magnitude standard deviation and confirm the absence of `level_std*` in the relevant line.  
3. Test the RandAugment functionality with different `level_std` values to observe the impact on the applied standard deviation.  
4. Document the observed results to confirm that the standard deviation remains fixed at 1.
