TITLE  
Uninitialized Bias Parameters in RelPositionMultiHeadedAttention Lead to Non-Deterministic Behavior

SYMPTOMS  
• The (u, v) bias terms in the Multi-Head Attention (MHA) layer are uninitialized, which can lead to non-deterministic behavior.  
• Observed outputs from uninitialized tensors can include unexpected values such as:  
  - `tensor(0.)` for a 1x1 tensor  
  - `tensor(4.9312e-38)` for a 10x10 tensor  
  - `tensor(nan)` for a 100x100 tensor  
• The model may produce NaN values during training or inference due to poor initialization.  
• The behavior is inconsistent and can vary between runs, leading to unreliable model performance.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The bias parameters (u, v) in the MHA layer should be properly initialized to zeros using `torch.zeros`, ensuring consistent and deterministic behavior during model training and inference.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Import the necessary libraries and modules.  
2. Instantiate the `RelPositionMultiHeadedAttention` class.  
3. Call the forward method with sample input data.  
4. Observe the outputs and note any NaN values or unexpected results.  
5. Check the initialization of the bias parameters by printing their values.  
6. Compare the outputs across multiple runs to confirm non-deterministic behavior.
