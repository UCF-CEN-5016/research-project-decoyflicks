TITLE  
Validation accuracy exceeds training accuracy during model training

SYMPTOMS  
• Validation accuracy consistently higher than training accuracy throughout training  
• Observed behavior occurs both with and without dropout and dropout_emb  
• No error messages presented; only unexpected behavior of accuracy metrics  

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The training accuracy should typically be equal to or higher than the validation accuracy, especially as training progresses. A model should not exhibit consistently higher validation accuracy than training accuracy unless there are specific reasons (e.g., data leakage, overfitting on training data).

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Use the provided example script to train the ViT model on the cats and dogs dataset.  
2. Set the model parameters as specified:  
   - batch_size = 64  
   - image_size = 224  
   - patch_size = 16  
   - num_classes = 2  
   - dim = 1024  
   - depth = 6  
   - heads = 16  
   - mlp_dim = 2048  
   - dropout = 0.1  
   - emb_dropout = 0.1  
3. Monitor and plot the training and validation accuracy during the training process.  
4. Compare the accuracy metrics to observe the discrepancies between training and validation accuracy.  
5. Repeat the training process with dropout and dropout_emb both enabled and disabled to confirm the issue persists.
