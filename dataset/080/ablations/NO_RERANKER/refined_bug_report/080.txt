TITLE  
Validation accuracy exceeds training accuracy during model training

SYMPTOMS  
• Validation accuracy is consistently higher than training accuracy throughout the training process.  
• This behavior occurs regardless of the use of dropout or dropout_emb.  
• No error messages are reported; the issue is related to the accuracy metrics.  
• The phenomenon is observed specifically with the ViT model on the cats and dogs dataset.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR  
The training accuracy should typically be equal to or higher than the validation accuracy during training, as the model is evaluated on the training data it has seen.

REPRODUCTION STEPS  
1. Use the example script provided for training on the cats and dogs dataset.  
2. Implement the ViT model with the specified parameters:  
   ```python
   from vit_pytorch.vit_for_small_dataset import ViT
   batch_size = 64
   model = ViT(
       image_size = 224,
       patch_size = 16,
       num_classes = 2,
       dim = 1024,
       depth = 6,
       heads = 16,
       mlp_dim = 2048,
       dropout = 0.1,
       emb_dropout = 0.1
   )
   ```  
3. Train the model and monitor both training and validation accuracy metrics throughout the training process.  
4. Compare the accuracy metrics to observe the discrepancies.  
5. Plot the training and validation accuracy for visual reference.
